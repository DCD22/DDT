Learning to drive

For some people, learning to drive can seem like an unachievable goal. Similar to learning to fly a plane or becoming the captain of a ship. For others it's as easy as a walk in the park or riding a bike


The important thing to remember is very simple - Everyone learns at a different pace and in different ways. Some people learn by reading manuals and the theory behind how and why things work, others learn by getting stuck in and improving through making mistakes. There's also the people who learn by watching others and putting in to practice what they've seen.


Picking the correct driving instructor for your learning style will help you improve and progress much faster than trying to adjust your learning technique. There's nothing wrong with shopping around for the perfect fit!

Types of vehicles

There are many different types of vehicles you can gain a licence for. It's important to note, in most countries you must already hold a driving licence for a car before you can gain any more vocational licences. Here's a list of all the different types of vocational licences you can gain.

  • Car
  • Motorcycle
  • HGV - Articulated goods vehicles
  • LGV - Rigid goods vehicles
  • PCV - Passenger carrying vehicles such as buses and coaches
